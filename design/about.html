Frequently asked questions

Why do both people have to be online to send files?

To receive encrypted files you need a personal key which must be kept private. To make tell now easy and secure a new key is generated every time you use it and is valid until you leave it. Thus, you don’t need to worry about keys anymore!

Why do I have to create a new secure channel every time I use tell now, even with the same person?

Our service is completely anonymous. No information about your identity is stored on our servers.

I heard that my Gmail is already encrypted, why should I use tell now?

Google only encrypts the traffic between you and their servers. There they have full access to your files. In fact this applies to almost any email provider. To make it even worse, their servers are often located in other countries, where privacy laws are less strict. tell now uses end-to-end encryption, so even we can’t read your files.

What is the “Encryption Keys” button for?

For extremely important documents, you have the possibility to use an additional security measure: You can compare your key with the one of your contact by phone. This prevents the insecurity of a so called man-in-the-middle attack, where somebody would steal your key to de- and reencrypt your messages. However, this is very unlikely and not relevant for the average user.

What’s the technology behind tell now?

tell now is a browser-based JavaScript webapp, using the secure OpenPGP protocol. tell now is open-source. The file transfer uses Binary.js.

Does it work with any browser?

Currently tell now is tested on Chrome, Safari and Firefox. However on Safari only specific file types like pdf, jpg, png and mp3 can be received, due to technical restrictions.
